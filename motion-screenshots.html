🌊 Fluid Simulation - Motion Visualization

Note: These are static visualizations showing what the fluid looks like at different time points. The actual simulation is a continuous, smooth 60 FPS animation where particles constantly move, swirl, and blend together.

Initial State (t=0s)

Particles start clustered in a spherical formation. Ready to begin fluid motion with curl noise physics.

Early Motion (t=2s)

Particles begin swirling outward in spiral patterns. Curl noise creates vortex formations with rising motion.

Active Flow (t=5s)

Complex turbulent patterns emerge. Particles form streams and eddies with continuous circular flow.

Steady State (t=10s)

Fully developed fluid motion. Particles continuously circulate, creating natural-looking turbulence.

High Turbulence

With turbulence slider at maximum. Chaotic, energetic motion with rapid swirling and complex patterns.

Large Particle Size

Particles at maximum size. Creates blob-like, cohesive fluid appearance with strong metaball blending.

To See the Full Animation:

  1. Open index.html in your browser
  2. Or start a local server: python -m http.server 8000
  3. Navigate to http://localhost:8000
  4. The fluid will animate at 60 FPS continuously
  5. Use mouse to orbit camera and see the 3D fluid from any angle
