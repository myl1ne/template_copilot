## Observable Evolutionary Phenomena

### 1. Natural Selection in Action
- Fitness-based reproduction creates clear generational improvement
- Unsuccessful strategies eliminated within 1-2 generations
- Successful adaptations spread rapidly through population

### 2. Convergent Evolution
- Multiple lineages arrive at similar solutions independently
- Vision-guided foraging becomes universal strategy
- Optimal body configurations repeatedly discovered

### 3. Genetic Drift vs Selection
- Neutral traits (e.g., exact color values) show drift
- Functional traits (e.g., vision sensor usage) show strong selection
- Balance creates both stability and diversity

### 4. Emergent Complexity
- No hardcoded foraging behavior
- Complex strategies emerge from simple rules:
  - Neural network (9 inputs → hidden → 3 outputs)
  - Physics constraints
  - Energy metabolism
  - Vision sensors
